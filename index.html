There are numerous braiding techniques, including simple three-strand braids, cornrows, box braids, and more complex styles.

In many cultures, braiding is a social activity, with family members and friends braiding each other's hair, fostering connection and passing down traditions.

Braids can protect hair from damage by keeping it out of the way and reducing manipulation, making them a popular choice for various hair types.

While braids themselves don't directly promote hair growth, they can create a protective environment that allows hair to grow longer and healthier by reducing breakage and tension.
